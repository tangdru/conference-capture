# Personas: Conference Capture

## Persona Map

| Persona | Type | Key condition |
|---------|------|---------------|
| Marcus | Primary | Situational — one-handed, high cognitive load day 2 |
| Priya | Primary | Presbyopia, wrist pain, slower mobile typist |
| James | Primary | ADHD — burst capture, high distractibility |
| Aiko | Edge case | Deaf — visual-only, relies on post-session transcript |
| Diane | Stress case | Temp motor impairment, fatigue, back-to-back sessions |

---

## Marcus

**Context:** 34, product strategist at a mid-size tech company. Attends 6–8 conferences a year across design, product, and AI. Flies in, hits 4–5 talks a day, debrief with his team on Monday. Conference capture is a professional habit — he's tried Notion, Otter, Apple Notes, and still cobbles together three apps.

**Abilities and conditions:** No permanent conditions. Situationally: always has a bag on one shoulder and often a coffee in the other hand. High cognitive load by day 2 of a multi-day conference — note quality drops.

**Technology:** iPhone 15, comfortable with apps, moderate-to-high technical confidence. Doesn't read documentation.

**Goals:** Capture enough to run a team debrief without rebuilding everything from memory. Get a shareable output with minimal post-processing.

**Frustrations:** Losing the thread between what he typed, what he photographed, and what the speaker actually said. Spending Sunday evening building a deck he should have had Friday.

**Environment:** Dark auditorium, seated in the middle of a row. Phone mostly under the seat-back table. Can't make noise.

## Priya

**Context:** 52, academic researcher in computational biology. Attends 10–12 conferences a year — mix of large industry events and small academic workshops. Takes detailed notes for literature reviews and grant proposals. Thinks in citations and methodology, not bullet points.

**Abilities and conditions:** Reading glasses for small text (presbyopia). Slower typing on mobile — prefers desktop but doesn't always have it. Occasional wrist pain from repetitive strain.

**Technology:** Android (Pixel), moderate technical confidence. Prefers things that are legible and don't require learning new patterns. Skeptical of AI features until they prove themselves.

**Goals:** Capture speaker arguments and methodology precisely. Know which paper they cited, who the speaker is, and what their institution affiliation is. Have a record she can cite later.

**Frustrations:** Speaker mentions a paper in passing — she can't write fast enough to get the full title. Ends up with half-captured references she has to reconstruct later. Mobile apps assume she's younger and faster.

**Environment:** Mix of well-lit academic rooms and dark keynote halls. Often has her laptop open alongside her phone.

## James

**Context:** 28, UX designer at a startup. Attends conferences for inspiration and community — less about note-taking, more about capturing sparks. Has ADHD, diagnosed at 22. Engages intensely for 10–15 minute bursts then loses the thread.

**Abilities and conditions:** ADHD — high distractibility, variable focus, impulsive capture behaviour. Hyperfocuses on interesting talks, completely disengages from boring ones.

**Technology:** iPhone, highly tech-literate, impatient with friction. Will abandon an app mid-session if it gets in his way.

**Goals:** Capture the moments of genuine interest without breaking the spell. Not drown in his own notes during review.

**Frustrations:** Opening an app and being asked to do anything before he can start. Missing the spark because the interface got in the way. Reviewing notes that are a chaotic mess with no way to find the good bits.

**Environment:** Everywhere — main stage, hallway conversations, lobby. Capture can happen anywhere.

---

## Aiko

**Context:** 41, deaf product manager (deaf from birth, BSL user). Attends conferences with a mix of BSL interpreters and live captioning — provision varies wildly by event. Often the only deaf attendee.

**Abilities and conditions:** Deaf. Uses BSL interpreter when provided. Relies on live captions (CART or auto-captions) when no interpreter is available. Excellent visual attention. Cannot rely on audio cues of any kind.

**Technology:** iPhone with VoiceOver off (she's sighted). Comfortable with accessibility features from a lifetime of adapting.

**Goals:** Capture what's happening visually — slides, speaker expressions, room reactions. Have a post-session transcript she can read to fill gaps when live captions were poor. Know who the speaker is and find their written work.

**Frustrations:** Audio-first tools that assume hearing. Live captions that disappear before she can read them. Missing 40% of content when captioning is poor with no way to recover it.

**Environment:** Front rows (better interpreter sight lines), or wherever captions are visible. Often photographing the caption screen as a workaround.

## Diane — Stress Case

**Context:** 45, venture capitalist. Attends 3–4 conferences a week during peak season. Flew in last night, meetings from 7am, five back-to-back talks today, dinner with LPs tonight. Wrist in a brace from a skiing injury (dominant hand).

**Abilities and conditions:** Temporary motor impairment (wrist brace, right hand). Fatigue. Back-to-back sessions with no buffer. Attention split between the talk and her inbox.

**Technology:** iPhone, high technical confidence, zero patience. If something takes more than one tap she will not use it.

**Goals:** Capture the signal from each talk without stopping between sessions. Know which companies and founders were mentioned. Have something she can forward to her associate before the flight home.

**Frustrations:** Having to close one session and set up another. Apps that need two hands. Anything that requires reading instructions. Losing content because she forgot to save.

**Environment:** Everywhere, always moving. High ambient noise. Checks her phone while walking. Exhausted by 3pm.

## Scenario Intersections

| Situation | Personas sharing it | Universal design opportunity |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| One-handed capture | Diane (wrist brace), Marcus (coffee), James (bag) | Every interaction must work one-handed — this is the default condition, not an edge case |
| Poor audio access | Aiko (deaf), Priya (loud room, unfamiliar accents) | Post-session transcript is valuable for everyone, not just deaf users |
| Cognitive overload during capture | James (ADHD), Diane (fatigue), Marcus (day 2) | Zero-friction capture protects all of them — any overhead costs the moment |
| Small text in poor lighting | Priya (presbyopia), everyone in a dark auditorium | Minimum text size and contrast must hold in dark mode |
| Losing references mid-talk | Priya (citations), Aiko (captions disappear), James (spark fades) | AI enrichment that recovers what they couldn't write down is universally valuable |
